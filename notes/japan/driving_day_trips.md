# Tokyo GR Supra Day-Trip Research

Research guide for **Wednesday, April 29, 2026** using the **GR Supra RZ** rental from `7-1 Gobancho, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo`.

## How To Read This Guide

- These routes are grouped by **total outing length**, not just driving time.
- Because **4/29 is Showa Day / Golden Week**, the shorter buckets are generally more reliable than the longer ones.
- The **10-hour** bucket is the hardest one to execute cleanly with an **8:00 AM pickup** because you lose the pre-rush escape window.
- The **10+ hour** bucket is where the strongest full-day experiences live if you are comfortable using almost the whole rental.

## At A Glance

### At A Glance: 6-Hour Routes

Best for a shorter outing with just enough time to get out of Tokyo, do one strong driving segment, hit a few worthwhile stops, and return without burning the whole rental day.

- **Mount Tsukuba / Purple Line loop**  
  Best short mountain-drive option. You get the Purple Line, Asahi Pass, Tsutsujigaoka, optional ropeway access, and a very car-culture-coded route without needing a full-day commitment.
- **Short Boso / Chiba interior loop**  
  Best short coastal-hills alternative. You get Umihotaru, Kanozan / Kujukutani views, and the Momiji Road / Boso Skyline feel, but you are more exposed to Aqua-Line holiday risk.

### At A Glance: 8-9 Hour Routes

Best for the **sweet spot**: enough time for a proper day out, but not so much that the whole rental becomes a marathon. These are the most balanced routes for combining fun road time with several worthwhile stops spread throughout the day.

- **Izu ridge-line / Turnpike loop**  
  Best premium-road option. This is the polished, driver-first route: Turnpike climb, ridge driving, and strong Supra-friendly tarmac, with optional ridge-top add-ons that can stretch the day toward **5:00-5:30 PM**.
- **Okutama alpine perimeter / Yanagisawa Pass loop**  
  Best mountain-balance option. This gives you alpine-feeling roads, Yanagisawa views, and good food stops, plus realistic optional detours that can extend the route without making it feel tacked on.
- **Boso Hidden Loop / Chiba interior**  
  Best coastal-hills alternative if you want a route that feels visually different from the mountain buckets. It spreads the stops well, but the Aqua-Line remains the built-in risk.
- **Doshi-michi / Lake Miyagase / Fuji foothills**  
  Best “rhythm road” alternative if you want a full day of steady flowing roads, lakeside pauses, and Fuji foothill views without committing to the full Fuji Five Lakes bucket.

### At A Glance: 10-Hour Routes

Best for a full-day route that feels more destination-driven. On 4/29 these are the trickiest to execute with an 8:00 AM pickup, so the versions here are intentionally compact.

- **Compact north-Fuji / Fuji Five Lakes day**  
  Best Fuji scenery play if visibility is good. This version trims the earlier full loop down to the strongest west-lake and pass sections.
- **Compact Nikko / Irohazaka / Lake Chuzenji day**  
  Best alpine-road classic. This keeps the famous Nikko road and lake but trims out slower extras so it has a chance of staying near a 10-hour day.

### At A Glance: 10+ Hour Routes

Best for using almost the whole rental. These are the strongest “big day out” routes and the ones that feel most worth the trouble if you want the car to be the center of the day.

- **Gunma / Usui / Karuizawa / Haruna full day**  
  Best overall long-day route. The mix is excellent: iconic pass driving, unusual volcanic scenery, Haruna lake roads, and strong regional food.
- **Okutama deep loop full day**  
  Best lower-stress long-day route. This is the mountain-immersion option: gorge, dam, lake, floating bridge, scenic road, and pass, all without quite the same expressway burden as Gunma.

## 6-Hour Routes

### 6h Option 1 — Mount Tsukuba / Purple Line Loop

**Best for:** the strongest short mountain-drive option that still feels properly car-focused.

#### 6h Option 1 route shape

`Gobancho -> Joban Expressway -> Tsuchiura-Kita IC -> Asahi Pass -> Omote-Tsukuba Skyline / Purple Line -> Tsutsujigaoka -> return to Tokyo`

#### 6h Option 1 totals

- **Total trip time:** ~6h 00m
- **Total driving time:** ~3h 45m
- **Approx distance:** ~175 km / 108.7 mi
- **Est. rental total:** **JPY 58,600 / $367.73**

#### 6h Option 1 timed plan

- **8:00-9:30 AM** drive from Gobancho to the Tsukuba side.
- **9:30-9:50 AM** **Asahi Pass** photo / reset stop.
- **9:50-10:15 AM** drive the **Purple Line / Omote-Tsukuba Skyline**.
- **10:15-11:00 AM** **Tsutsujigaoka Rest House** stop.
- **11:00-11:45 AM** optional ropeway / summit view time.
- **11:45 AM-2:00 PM** return to Tokyo with a light buffer.

#### 6h Option 1 strengths

- Very good road quality for a short outing.
- Strong car-culture feel without having to drive half the day just to get there.
- Less overlapped with your Tokyo / Hakone / Kyoto stays than the obvious tourist plays.

#### 6h Option 1 main 4/29 risks

- Joban Expressway still gets holiday traffic, just not as brutally as Tomei or Chuo.
- The 6-hour window is tight enough that a lazy departure kills the route.
- Route 42 base-area congestion is why the **Purple Line detour** matters.

#### 6h Option 1 stop details

##### Asahi Pass

- **What it offers:** One of the classic local car-meet style viewpoints in the Tsukuba area.
- **Time at stop:** ~20 minutes.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** Supra hero shots with a wide Kanto Plain backdrop.
- **Practical / Supra note:** Flat enough entry and easy parking.

##### Omote-Tsukuba Skyline / Purple Line

- **What it offers:** The main driving payoff of the route: 12 km of winding road with a strong local enthusiast reputation.
- **Time at stop:** ~25 minutes as a driving segment.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** The actual drive itself. This is the reason to choose Tsukuba.
- **Practical / Supra note:** Better surface and cleaner rhythm than trying to improvise around the smaller base roads.

##### Tsutsujigaoka Rest House

- **What it offers:** Easy stop with views, food, and optional ropeway access.
- **Time at stop:** ~45-90 minutes depending on whether you add the ropeway.
- **Cost:** Parking about **JPY 500 / $3.14**; optional ropeway about **JPY 1,300 round-trip / $8.16** for adults.
- **Best use:** Coffee, snack, short summit option, and a clean turn-around point.
- **Food:** Rest-house snacks and simple light meal options.
- **Practical / Supra note:** Use the easier lower parking terrace if the lot is busy.

### 6h Option 2 — Short Boso / Chiba Interior Loop

**Best for:** a lower-commitment “different scenery” option if you want bay views plus some inland curves.

#### 6h Option 2 route shape

`Gobancho -> Aqua-Line -> Umihotaru -> Kanozan / Kujukutani -> Momiji Road / Boso Skyline area -> Nomizo Falls -> return to Tokyo`

#### 6h Option 2 totals

- **Total trip time:** ~6h 00m
- **Total driving time:** ~3h 30m
- **Approx distance:** ~140 km / 87.0 mi
- **Est. rental total:** **JPY 58,600 / $367.73**

#### 6h Option 2 timed plan

- **8:00-9:15 AM** drive from Gobancho to **Umihotaru**.
- **9:15-9:45 AM** Umihotaru stop.
- **9:45-10:30 AM** climb toward the **Kanozan / Kujukutani** side.
- **10:30-11:00 AM** Kujukutani view stop.
- **11:00-11:45 AM** drive the **Momiji Road / Boso interior** segment.
- **11:45 AM-12:45 PM** **Nomizo Falls** plus quick lunch.
- **12:45-2:00 PM** return sprint to Tokyo before Aqua-Line inbound traffic worsens.

#### 6h Option 2 strengths

- Feels totally different from the mountain routes.
- Easier on the car in terms of road roughness and elevation.
- Gives you one engineering-landmark stop (`Umihotaru`) and one scenic-inland driving section.

#### 6h Option 2 main 4/29 risks

- The **Aqua-Line** is the entire risk case.
- If traffic is already ugly at departure, this route gets downgraded fast.
- This is a good short route only if you stay disciplined and do not drift deeper into Boso.

#### 6h Option 2 stop details

##### Umihotaru (8-9h Boso route)

- **What it offers:** The most distinctive “Tokyo Bay” stop possible, with 360-degree sea views and a surreal mid-bay setting.
- **Time at stop:** ~30 minutes.
- **Cost:** Stop itself is free; the real cost is the Aqua-Line tolls already baked into the drive.
- **Best use:** Coffee, sea-air reset, and car photos.
- **Food:** Bakery / coffee / snack options are easy here.

##### Kujukutani Observatory Park

- **What it offers:** Layered Chiba hill views and one of the better “unexpectedly scenic” overlooks near Tokyo.
- **Time at stop:** ~30 minutes.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** Fast viewpoint stop before the inland driving segment.
- **Practical / Supra note:** Paved lot, but enter slowly.

##### Momiji Road / Boso Skyline area

- **What it offers:** The main fun-road segment in this bucket.
- **Time at stop:** ~30-45 minutes as a driving segment.
- **Cost:** Generally free on the drive segment itself used here.
- **Best use:** Smooth, flowing, lower-stress road time.
- **Practical / Supra note:** Less “mountain pass” than Tsukuba or Okutama, but still enjoyable.

##### Nomizo Falls

- **What it offers:** Short walk to the cave-and-waterfall scene that gets the “Ghibli” nickname.
- **Time at stop:** ~45-60 minutes with a quick lunch nearby.
- **Cost:** Free for the falls.
- **Best use:** One final proper stop before heading straight back.
- **Food:** Quick soba or light local lunch nearby works better than trying to build a long sit-down meal into this bucket.

## 8-9 Hour Routes

### 8-9h Option 1 — Izu Ridge-Line / Turnpike Loop

**Best for:** the most premium, polished “sports-car road” experience in the guide.

#### 8-9h Option 1 route shape

`Gobancho -> Tomei -> Odawara-Atsugi -> Hakone Turnpike -> Daikanyama / Sky Lounge -> Izu Skyline -> Skyport Kameishi -> reverse back to Tokyo`

#### 8-9h Option 1 totals

- **Core total trip time:** ~8h 00m
- **Core total driving time:** ~4h 30m to 5h 00m
- **Approx distance:** ~215 km / 133.6 mi
- **Est. rental total:** **JPY 59,800 / $375.26**
- **With optional add-ons:** can naturally stretch to about **5:00-5:30 PM** return if traffic / energy / weather all cooperate.

#### 8-9h Option 1 timed plan

- **8:00-9:15 AM** drive from Gobancho to the Odawara side.
- **9:15-9:30 AM** very quick service-area / traffic-check stop if needed.
- **9:30-10:30 AM** climb onto the **Hakone Turnpike**.
- **10:30-11:00 AM** **Anest Iwata Sky Lounge / Daikanyama** stop.
- **11:00 AM-12:15 PM** **Izu Skyline** drive.
- **12:15-1:00 PM** **Skyport Kameishi** lunch / reset stop.
- **1:00-1:45 PM** one more ridge-line run northbound.
- **1:45-4:00 PM** return to Tokyo before the heavier evening wave.

#### 8-9h Option 1 strengths

- Best pure tarmac quality in the guide.
- Feels like a driving event rather than a sightseeing day.
- Even though you are staying in Hakone, this route still makes sense because it is built around the **road itself**, not around Hakone sightseeing.

#### 8-9h Option 1 main 4/29 risks

- The approach is the problem, not the road once you are there.
- Tomei / Odawara-Atsugi can get ugly very quickly on 4/29.
- This route becomes weak if you do not get out of Tokyo promptly.

#### 8-9h Option 1 stop details

##### Hakone Turnpike / Daikanyama Sky Lounge

- **What it offers:** One of Japan’s classic sports-car climbs plus the iconic high viewpoint.
- **Time at stop:** ~30-45 minutes.
- **Cost:** Expect the main Turnpike toll to be roughly **JPY 730-900 (~$4.58-$5.65)** depending on exact segment used.
- **Best use:** The climb, the view, and the photos.
- **Practical / Supra note:** Smooth enough for the Supra; take entries and speed bumps cleanly.

##### Izu Skyline / Skyport Kameishi

- **What it offers:** Long high-speed sweepers, ridge driving, and a proper halfway reset point.
- **Time at stop:** ~45 minutes at Kameishi; ~60-75 minutes including the main drive segment.
- **Cost:** A representative Izu Skyline segment is about **JPY 640 / $4.02**; route-level tolls are the main cost driver here.
- **Best use:** Ridge driving first, quick lunch second.
- **Food:** Wasabi soft serve or light meal options if open.
- **Practical / Supra note:** Flat enough parking and great space for a low car.

#### 8-9h Option 1 optional stretch stops

Choose **one or two** of these to make the day feel fuller without dropping into ugly low-level holiday traffic.

##### Jukkokutoge / Tengoku Café

- **What it offers:** A very clean ridge-top add-on with cable car access, panoramic views, and a proper café stop.
- **Added time:** ~45-60 minutes.
- **Cost:** Cable car about **JPY 730 / $4.58** round trip; café snacks / drinks are extra.
- **Where it fits:** Best immediately after the northern Izu Skyline segment.
- **Why it works:** Gives the day another real stop in the middle-to-late phase instead of just “turn around and go home.”

##### Mt. Gen Observatory

- **What it offers:** A quick, photo-friendly micro-detour with a wide paved pull-off and broad Izu/Suruga views.
- **Added time:** ~15-20 minutes.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Where it fits:** Between **Skyport Kameishi** and the northern Izu Skyline exit.
- **Why it works:** Minimal time cost but makes the afternoon feel less front-loaded.

##### Ashinoko Skyline / Mikuni Pass

- **What it offers:** One last classic Hakone / Fuji / Lake Ashi viewpoint on a smooth scenic toll road.
- **Added time:** ~30 minutes.
- **Cost:** About **JPY 1,000 / $6.28** for a standard car over the main section.
- **Where it fits:** Best after **Jukkokutoge** and before fully committing to the return.
- **Caveat:** This is a separate toll road, so only do it if the day is still ahead of schedule.

##### EXPASA Ashigara (Inbound)

- **What it offers:** The most practical “wait out traffic with dignity” stop on the way home.
- **Added time:** ~30-60 minutes, depending on how much traffic you want to absorb.
- **Cost:** Free to stop; figure about **JPY 1,000 / $6.28** for coffee / snacks.
- **Where it fits:** Once you are already committed to the Tomei inbound return.
- **Why it works:** If the return is turning ugly, this is the cleanest way to use the extra time.

#### 8-9h Option 4 stop details

##### Lake Miyagase / Toriibara

- **What it offers:** Large easy parking, lake views, and strong “car gathering” energy.
- **Time at stop:** ~30 minutes.
- **Cost:** Often free, though holiday parking can sometimes run about **JPY 500 / $3.14** depending on lot / period.
- **Best use:** First pause of the day and a natural reset after leaving Tokyo.

##### Doshi-michi / Route 413

- **What it offers:** The main road rhythm of the route.
- **Time at stop:** ~60-75 minutes as a driving segment.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** The drive itself, not a single overlook.
- **Why it works:** This is the route for someone who wants a steady, enjoyable road rather than a few huge hero stops.

##### Michi-no-Eki Doshi

- **What it offers:** Good middle-of-the-day roadside stop with river views and solid local-food energy.
- **Time at stop:** ~30 minutes.
- **Cost:** Snacks / light meal roughly **JPY 1,000 / $6.28**.
- **Best use:** Sweetfish, watercress specialties, coffee, and a quick leg stretch.

##### Yamanakako lunch area

- **What it offers:** Best place in this route for a proper meal plus a Fuji-facing atmosphere.
- **Time at stop:** ~45 minutes.
- **Cost:** Typical lunch roughly **JPY 1,500-2,500 / ~$9.41-$15.69**.
- **Best use:** Sit-down lunch before the scenic upper-lake stops.

##### Panorama-dai

- **What it offers:** Best elevated Fuji-and-lake viewpoint in this route family.
- **Time at stop:** ~20-30 minutes.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** Main scenic payoff before turning homeward.
- **Practical / Supra note:** Surface and entry conditions should be approached carefully in a low car.

##### Hirano no Hama area

- **What it offers:** Lower, lake-edge Fuji view and a calmer final stop.
- **Time at stop:** ~20-25 minutes.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** Final mood stop before accepting the return leg.

## 10-Hour Routes

### 10h Option 1 — Compact North Fuji / Deep Lakes Loop

**Best for:** the strongest Fuji-area option that still has a chance of staying near a 10-hour day.

#### 10h Option 1 route shape

`Gobancho -> Chuo -> Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya -> Oishi Park -> Lake Saiko / Nenba -> Narusawa Ice Cave -> Lake Motosu -> Lake Shoji -> Tokyo`

#### 10h Option 1 totals

- **Total trip time:** ~10h 00m target
- **Total driving time:** ~5h 15m
- **Approx distance:** ~250 km / 155.3 mi
- **Est. rental total:** **JPY 62,600 / $392.83**

#### 10h Option 1 timed plan

- **8:00-9:50 AM** drive to **Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya**.
- **9:50-10:20 AM** pass / tea-house / Fuji-view stop.
- **10:20-10:45 AM** move to **Oishi Park**.
- **10:45-11:10 AM** Oishi Park stop.
- **11:10 AM-12:00 PM** move to **Lake Saiko / Nenba**.
- **12:00-12:45 PM** Lake Saiko / lunch / Nenba choice.
- **12:45-1:10 PM** move to **Narusawa Ice Cave**.
- **1:10-1:35 PM** cave stop.
- **1:35-2:10 PM** move to **Lake Motosu**.
- **2:10-2:35 PM** Motosu / Shoji quick stops.
- **2:35-6:00 PM** return to Tokyo with a disciplined afternoon departure.

#### 10h Option 1 strengths

- It keeps the best parts of the earlier Fuji loop and drops weaker / slower extras.
- Best western-lakes Fuji mood without trying to do absolutely everything.
- Good if the weather is clear and you want a true “Fuji day.”

#### 10h Option 1 main 4/29 risks

- Weather and visibility.
- Chuo Expressway return timing.
- If you get sucked into Shibazakura or heavy Motosuko traffic, this route slips into the 10+ bucket quickly.

#### 10h Option 1 stop details

##### Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya

- **What it offers:** Historic tea-house stop with one of the best elevated Fuji / Kawaguchiko views.
- **Time at stop:** ~25-45 minutes.
- **Cost:** View stop is effectively free; tea / dango / light food spend is optional.
- **Best use:** Strongest built-in first stop in the Fuji bucket.

##### Oishi Park

- **What it offers:** Clean postcard-style Fuji framing.
- **Time at stop:** ~15-25 minutes.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** Fast, efficient photo stop.

##### Lake Saiko / Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba

- **What it offers:** The calmer, less-touristed side of the Fuji Five Lakes zone.
- **Time at stop:** ~20-45 minutes, or ~45-60 minutes if you add Nenba.
- **Cost:** Lake stop is free; **`Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba`** is about **JPY 500 / $3.14** for adults and **JPY 250 / $1.57** for children.
- **Best use:** Lunch and one stop that feels more like an activity than a viewpoint.

##### Narusawa Ice Cave

- **What it offers:** Weird, fun, specific Fuji-area lava-tube stop.
- **Time at stop:** ~20-30 minutes.
- **Cost:** About **JPY 350 / $2.20** for adults and **JPY 200 / $1.26** for children.
- **Best use:** Best bad-weather insurance stop in the Fuji bucket.

##### Lake Motosu / Lake Shoji

- **What it offers:** The deepest-lake view stop plus the quiet mood stop.
- **Time at stop:** ~10-20 minutes at Motosu and ~5-10 minutes at Shoji.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** Final western-lakes payoff before the return.

### 10h Option 2 — Compact Nikko / Irohazaka / Lake Chuzenji Day

**Best for:** the strongest alpine-road classic if you are willing to accept that 4/29 makes it fragile.

#### 10h Option 2 route shape

`Gobancho -> Tohoku Expressway -> Nikko -> Second Irohazaka -> Akechidaira -> Lake Chuzenji / Kegon -> Ryuzu Falls -> First Irohazaka -> Tokyo`

#### 10h Option 2 totals

- **Total trip time:** ~10h 00m target
- **Total driving time:** ~5h 45m
- **Approx distance:** ~300 km / 186.4 mi
- **Est. rental total:** **JPY 66,600 / $417.93**

#### 10h Option 2 timed plan

- **8:00-10:30 AM** drive from Gobancho to the Nikko side.
- **10:30-11:00 AM** **Second Irohazaka** ascent.
- **11:00-11:30 AM** **Akechidaira** stop.
- **11:30 AM-12:15 PM** **Lake Chuzenji / Kegon** area stop.
- **12:15-1:00 PM** quick lunch around Chuzenji.
- **1:00-1:20 PM** move to **Ryuzu Falls**.
- **1:20-1:40 PM** Ryuzu stop.
- **1:40-2:10 PM** descend the return Irohazaka route.
- **2:10-6:00 PM** return to Tokyo before the worst inbound wave.

#### 10h Option 2 strengths

- Irohazaka is still one of the canonical driving roads near Tokyo.
- Lake Chuzenji gives the route a clear destination anchor.
- Best alpine-feeling route in the guide outside the longer Gunma and Okutama plays.

#### 10h Option 2 main 4/29 risks

- Highest traffic risk in the document outside the most ambitious long days.
- This route is very sensitive to a slow morning escape from Tokyo.
- If Nikko is clogged, this route becomes an **11h+** route immediately.

#### 10h Option 2 stop details

##### Second Irohazaka

- **What it offers:** The actual driving reason to do Nikko.
- **Time at stop:** ~20-30 minutes as a driving segment.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** Enjoy the uphill hairpins; this is the core of the route.

##### Akechidaira

- **What it offers:** Best classic overview stop of the road / lake / falls complex.
- **Time at stop:** ~30 minutes.
- **Cost:** View area itself is free; the ropeway is a closure-risk item and should be verified before relying on it.
- **Best use:** Photo / overview stop rather than a long activity stop.

##### Lake Chuzenji / Kegon area

- **What it offers:** The elegant lunch-and-lake reset of the route.
- **Time at stop:** ~45-60 minutes including lunch.
- **Cost:** Kegon Falls elevator is about **JPY 600 / $3.77** if you choose it; parking often lands around **JPY 500-1,000 / ~$3.14-$6.28** depending on lot.
- **Best use:** Short sit-down lunch and a proper reset.

##### Ryuzu Falls

- **What it offers:** One of the best effort-to-reward waterfall stops in the guide.
- **Time at stop:** ~15-20 minutes.
- **Cost:** Free to view.
- **Best use:** Quick waterfall payoff before heading down.

## 10+ Hour Routes

I kept the **two strongest routes from the original long-day draft** for this section: the one with the best overall mix (**Gunma**) and the one with the best lower-stress mountain immersion (**Okutama deep**).

### 10+h Option 1 — Gunma / Usui / Karuizawa / Haruna Full Day

**Best for:** the most complete “I rented a Supra in Japan” day.

#### 10+h Option 1 route shape

`Gobancho -> Kan-Etsu -> Takasaka SA -> Megane Bridge -> Old Usui Pass -> Shiraito Falls -> Onioshidashi Park -> Lake Haruna -> Mizusawa / Takasaki side -> Tokyo`

#### 10+h Option 1 totals

- **Total trip time:** ~11h 00m
- **Total driving time:** ~6h 40m
- **Approx distance:** ~310 km / 192.6 mi
- **Est. rental total:** **JPY 67,400 / $422.95**

#### 10+h Option 1 timed plan

- **8:00-9:15 AM** drive to **Takasaka SA**.
- **9:15-9:30 AM** Takasaka reset stop.
- **9:30-10:25 AM** drive to **Megane Bridge**.
- **10:25-10:45 AM** Megane Bridge stop.
- **10:45-11:10 AM** Old Usui Pass approach.
- **11:10-11:30 AM** **Shiraito Falls** stop.
- **11:30 AM-12:05 PM** scenic drive via **Shiraito Highland Way / Kita-Karuizawa**.
- **12:05-12:40 PM** **Onioshidashi Park** stop.
- **12:40-1:50 PM** drive to **Lake Haruna**.
- **1:50-2:15 PM** lake stop.
- **2:15-3:00 PM** Mizusawa lunch.
- **3:00-7:00 PM** return leg with holiday-traffic buffer.

#### 10+h Option 1 strengths

- Best blend of special roads, special stops, and special food.
- Strongest all-around use of the car.
- Feels the most different from Tokyo, Hakone, and Kyoto.

#### 10+h Option 1 main 4/29 risks

- Longest outbound and inbound highway exposure in the guide.
- If return traffic starts stacking early, this route loses its margin.

#### 10+h Option 1 stop details

##### Takasaka SA

- **What it offers:** First reset stop and useful decision point.
- **Time at stop:** ~15-20 minutes.
- **Best use:** Coffee, fuel, snack, traffic check.
- **Food:** Miso bread or a pork-bowl style breakfast stop.

##### Usui 3rd Bridge (Megane-bashi)

- **What it offers:** Dramatic short architecture stop.
- **Time at stop:** ~15-25 minutes.
- **Best use:** Walk up, shoot photos, move on.

##### Old Usui Pass / Karuizawa side

- **What it offers:** The section that makes the route feel like a true mountain-drive day.
- **Time at stop:** ~10-20 minutes if you pause at the summit area.
- **Best use:** The drive itself plus a quick shrine / tea-house stretch.

##### Shiraito Falls

- **What it offers:** Broad curtain waterfall with very low effort.
- **Time at stop:** ~15-20 minutes.
- **Cost:** The falls are effectively free; the **Shiraito Highland Way** approach is about **JPY 500 one-way / $3.14**.
- **Best use:** Fast refresh stop and forest photos.

##### Onioshidashi Park

- **What it offers:** The most visually unusual stop on the route.
- **Time at stop:** ~30-45 minutes.
- **Cost:** About **JPY 700 / $4.39** for adults and **JPY 500 / $3.14** for children.
- **Best use:** Pay to walk the lava field and get the most distinctive photos of the day.

##### Lake Haruna

- **What it offers:** Scenic caldera lake and one more good road segment.
- **Time at stop:** ~15-30 minutes.
- **Cost:** Lake stop is free; optional **Harunasan Ropeway** is about **JPY 950 round-trip / $5.96** for adults.
- **Best use:** Quick lake reset or optional ropeway if time is unusually good.

##### Mizusawa / Takasaki side

- **What it offers:** The best meal stop in the entire guide.
- **Time at stop:** ~40-60 minutes.
- **Best use:** Proper **Mizusawa udon** lunch.
- **Order:** Cold udon with sesame dipping sauce plus tempura.

### 10+h Option 2 — Okutama Deep Loop Full Day

**Best for:** the most efficient full-use mountain day if you want a calmer long route.

#### 10+h Option 2 route shape

`Gobancho -> Hatonosu Gorge -> Shiromaru Dam -> Nippara area -> Lake Okutama / Mugiyama -> Okutama Shuyu Road -> Yanagisawa Pass -> Tokyo`

#### 10+h Option 2 totals

- **Total trip time:** ~11h 00m
- **Total driving time:** ~5h 30m
- **Approx distance:** ~220 km / 136.7 mi
- **Est. rental total:** **JPY 60,200 / $377.77**

#### 10+h Option 2 timed plan

- **8:00-9:30 AM** drive to **Hatonosu Gorge**.
- **9:30-9:50 AM** gorge stop.
- **9:50-10:10 AM** drive to **Shiromaru Dam**.
- **10:10-10:30 AM** dam stop.
- **10:30-11:35 AM** deeper west drive toward **Nippara / Lake Okutama**.
- **11:35 AM-12:20 PM** **Mugiyama Floating Bridge** area stop.
- **12:20-1:10 PM** **Okutama Shuyu Road / Tsukiyomi**.
- **1:10-2:30 PM** **Tabayama** lunch segment.
- **2:30-2:45 PM** **Yanagisawa Pass** stop.
- **2:45-7:00 PM** return with wide buffer.

#### 10+h Option 2 strengths

- Best actual-scenic-driving to total-day ratio.
- Lowest stress long-day option.
- Strong mountain texture without needing the same long expressway grind as Gunma.

#### 10+h Option 2 main 4/29 risks

- Nippara access can become annoying on a holiday.
- Not as iconic as Gunma if the goal is maximum “special route” energy.

#### 10+h Option 2 stop details

##### Hatonosu Gorge

- **What it offers:** Early scenic payoff with suspension bridge / river / rock walls.
- **Time at stop:** ~15-30 minutes.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** First real nature stop of the day.

##### Shiromaru Dam

- **What it offers:** Unusual industrial / landscape contrast and optional fish-ladder interest.
- **Time at stop:** ~15-25 minutes.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** Quick distinctive stop that still works in mixed weather.

##### Nippara Limestone Cave

- **What it offers:** Best optional paid “we actually did something” stop.
- **Time at stop:** ~35-60 minutes.
- **Cost:** About **JPY 900 / $5.65** for adults, **JPY 700 / $4.39** for junior high students, and **JPY 600 / $3.77** for children.
- **Best use:** Only if access traffic is behaving.

##### Mugiyama Floating Bridge

- **What it offers:** Playful, memorable Lake Okutama stop.
- **Time at stop:** ~15-20 minutes.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** Walk it, photograph it, move on.

##### Okutama Shuyu Road / Tsukiyomi Daiichi Parking

- **What it offers:** The driving heart of the deep loop.
- **Time at stop:** ~10-20 minutes at the overlook, plus the road itself.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Best use:** Lake overlook and strong car-photo stop.

##### Tabayama / roadside station area

- **What it offers:** Best long-route lunch anchor.
- **Time at stop:** ~30-45 minutes.
- **Cost:** Typical lunch around **JPY 1,200-1,500 / ~$7.53-$9.41**.
- **Food:** Venison / gibier dishes are the best reason to stop.

##### Yanagisawa Pass

- **What it offers:** The high-point atmosphere stop.
- **Time at stop:** ~10-15 minutes.
- **Cost:** Free to park; tea / snack spend is optional.
- **Best use:** Quick view and air-change moment before the way home.

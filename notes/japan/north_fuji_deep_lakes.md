# North Fuji / Deep Lakes Loop

A standalone route guide for a **Wednesday, April 29, 2026** GR Supra day drive from `7-1 Gobancho, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo`.

This is the **Compact North Fuji / Deep Lakes Loop** from the main guide, expanded into a single-route plan you could realistically follow on the day.

## Why This Drive Is Awesome

- This is one of the most **beautifully varied Fuji days** you can do by car.
- The day keeps changing:
  - elevated Fuji-and-lake drama at `Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya`
  - classic postcard framing at `Oishi Park`
  - quieter, more rural Fuji atmosphere at `Lake Saiko / Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba`
  - a weird lava-tube detour at `Narusawa Ice Cave`
  - the iconic `Lake Motosu` banknote view
  - a final calm mood stop at `Lake Shoji`
- It feels more layered than a standard Kawaguchiko day because it is not just “park, look at Fuji, repeat.”
- It combines:
  - one of the best elevated Fuji viewpoints
  - one of the prettiest lakeside viewpoints
  - one real paid cultural stop
  - one geological stop
  - and two of the quietest lakes in the Fuji Five Lakes area

If the weather cooperates, this is one of the strongest **sightseeing-first driving days** in the whole guide.

## The Big Caveat

This route is only worth doing if **two things are at least cooperating**:

1. the **Chuo Expressway** is not completely broken
2. **Fuji visibility** is at least decent for part of the day

This is **Showa Day / Golden Week**, so:

- outbound traffic toward the Fuji area can get heavy
- the return on the Chuo can get ugly if you leave too late
- Fuji can disappear behind cloud or haze and flatten the emotional payoff of multiple stops

If the outbound Chuo situation already looks ugly when you are ready to leave Tokyo, this is the route I would seriously reconsider.

## Fuji Route Risk: Best Case, Medium Case, Worst Case

This route has two separate risk variables:

- **road traffic**
- **mountain visibility**

### Best Case

- **What it looks like:** Chuo is moving well enough, and Fuji is visible at least through the morning and midday.
- **What that means for the day:** The route is excellent and feels close to the ideal version.
- **Likely effect on schedule:** You can keep the full stop sequence and still come back feeling like the day was worth the effort.
- **How to use this case:** Commit to the full route and enjoy the stops without rushing unnecessarily.

### Medium Case

- **What it looks like:** Chuo is slow but moving, or Fuji is visible only intermittently.
- **What that means for the day:** The route is still good, but you need discipline.
- **Likely effect on schedule:** One stop needs to stay tight, and the route may drift later than the ideal return.
- **How to use this case:** Keep `Oishi Park` efficient, make one clear lunch decision, and do not add random extras after `Lake Motosu / Lake Shoji`.

### Worst Case

- **What it looks like:** Chuo is badly jammed early, Fuji is fully socked in, or both.
- **What that means for the day:** The route loses the main reason it exists.
- **Likely effect on schedule:** You spend too much time getting there and not enough time enjoying the actual payoff.
- **How to use this case:** Drop the route rather than forcing it.

## Fuji Route Decision Rule

Use this simple framework on the morning of the drive:

- **If Chuo looks manageable and Fuji visibility looks decent:** Run the full route.
- **If Chuo looks slow but still workable:** Run it, but keep the stop timings disciplined.
- **If Chuo already looks bad leaving Tokyo:** Drop the route.
- **If Fuji visibility is very poor all morning:** Only do this if you still want the scenic-drive / food / village / cave combo enough to justify the effort.

## Route Shape

`Gobancho -> Chuo -> Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya -> Oishi Park -> Lake Saiko / Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba -> Narusawa Ice Cave -> Lake Motosu -> Lake Shoji -> return to Tokyo`

## What This Day Feels Like

This is not the route you choose for the purest driving road.  
It is the route you choose when you want a **beautiful, layered Fuji day** where the stops are strong enough to justify the effort.

The rhythm of the day is:

1. Leave Tokyo and climb toward an elevated Fuji viewpoint
2. Get the classic “Fuji + lake” framing early
3. Move into a quieter and more atmospheric village/lakeside section
4. Add one weird volcanic / cave stop so the day is not all viewpoints
5. Finish with the deepest and quietest west-side lakes

## How We Actually Run The Day

### Before leaving

- Fuel up early if needed.
- Bring cash plus card.
- Bring a light layer; the lakes and pass can feel meaningfully cooler than central Tokyo.
- Check Fuji visibility before you commit.
- Keep navigation open the whole time because this route’s success depends on the Chuo timing.

### On the drive

- `Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya` should be treated as the emotional opening move of the route.
- `Oishi Park` is a quick, efficient Fuji-photo stop, not a place to lose an hour.
- `Lake Saiko / Nenba` is where the route becomes more atmospheric and less tourist-obvious.
- `Narusawa Ice Cave` is your weird mid-day reset.
- `Lake Motosu` is your big western-lakes payoff.
- `Lake Shoji` is your final quiet mood stop.

## Stop-By-Stop Guide

## Stop 1 — Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya

![Tenka-chaya at Misaka Pass](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/recover/images/the-teahouse-in-the-sky-misaka-pass.jpg)

_Image source: [The Teahouse in the Sky, Misaka Pass](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/recover/teahouse-in-the-sky.html)_

- **What it offers:** One of the best elevated Fuji-and-lake views on the entire north side.
- **Time at stop:** about **25-45 minutes**
- **Cost:** The stop itself is effectively free; tea / dango / hoto spend is optional.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Pull in, get the first big view of Fuji over Kawaguchiko, walk a little, and either have tea or go straight into a light early meal.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  It is one of the few stops in the guide that feels scenic, historic, and literary at the same time.
- **What makes it unique:**  
  This is the `Osamu Dazai / Fugaku Hyakkei` stop, not just a random Fuji overlook.
- **Best food move:**  
  Tea and something light, or hoto if you want to make this your main meal stop.
- **Practical / Supra notes:**  
  Stay calm on the pass road and do not rush the approach. This is a scenic stop first, not a place to drive heroically.

## Stop 2 — Oishi Park

![Oishi Park and Mount Fuji](https://en.kawaguchiko.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/0410.jpg)

_Image source: [Oishi Park official page](https://en.kawaguchiko.net/park-en/oishi-park/)_

- **What it offers:** The cleanest classic “Mt. Fuji + lake + foreground flowers / promenade” composition in the route.
- **Time at stop:** about **15-25 minutes**
- **Cost:** Free.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Park, walk out, take the obvious great photos, and leave before this becomes a time sink.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  It gives the route one polished postcard stop before you move into the quieter western lakes.
- **What makes it unique:**  
  It is the most instantly recognizable Fuji framing in the route.
- **Best food move:**  
  Quick soft serve or coffee only if you are ahead of schedule.
- **Practical / Supra notes:**  
  Parking is paved and easy, but this can get busy quickly on 4/29.

## Stop 3 — Lake Saiko / Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba

![Saiko Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba](https://saikoiyashinosatonenba.jp/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/DSC3030-1024x512.jpg)

_Image source: [Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba official site](https://saikoiyashinosatonenba.jp/en/aboutus/)_

- **What it offers:** The quieter and more atmospheric side of the Fuji Five Lakes zone.
- **Time at stop:** about **45-60 minutes** if you include `Nenba`, or **20-30 minutes** if you keep it lakeside only.
- **Cost:** Lake stop is free; **`Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba`** is about **JPY 500 / $3.14** for adults and **JPY 250 / $1.57** for children.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Use this as the middle-of-day anchor. Walk the village if you want a proper activity stop, then eat lunch nearby or inside the area depending on timing.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  This is where the route becomes more than “nice Fuji views.”
- **What makes it unique:**  
  You get a reconstructed thatched-roof village with Mt. Fuji atmosphere in the background, which is a very different feel from Kawaguchiko’s polished photo spots.
- **Best food move:**  
  If you want the route to feel brochure-worthy, this is one of the best places to slow down and actually eat.
- **Practical / Supra notes:**  
  This area is easier on the car than some rougher trailhead / shoulder parking situations, but still arrive calmly and park deliberately.

## Stop 4 — Narusawa Ice Cave

![Narusawa Ice Cave](https://www.mtfuji-cave.com/contents/common/images/ice_cave/title_image_en.jpg)

_Image source: [Narusawa Ice Cave official page](https://www.mtfuji-cave.com/en/ice_cave/)_

- **What it offers:** A strange, fun lava-tube stop that completely changes the texture of the day.
- **Time at stop:** about **20-30 minutes**
- **Cost:** About **JPY 350 / $2.20** for adults and **JPY 200 / $1.26** for children.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Park, go in, do the loop, and come back out feeling like you broke the rhythm of the route in the best possible way.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  Without this, the route becomes too view-heavy. This is the stop that makes the day more interesting.
- **What makes it unique:**  
  It is one of the rare stops in the guide that feels geological and tactile instead of purely scenic.
- **Practical / Supra notes:**  
  Good stop if Fuji is getting hazy. The cave still works even when visibility does not.

## Stop 5 — Lake Motosu

![Lake Motosu](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/discover/images/lake-motosu.jpg)

_Image source: [Lake Motosuko official guide](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/discover/lake-motosuko.html)_

- **What it offers:** The deepest and most iconic west-side lake mood in the route.
- **Time at stop:** about **15-25 minutes**
- **Cost:** Free.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Pull over, take in the view, and let this be the big west-lake payoff before heading home.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  This is the emotional “deep Fuji” moment of the route.
- **What makes it unique:**  
  It is tied to the 1,000-yen-bill image and feels more open, cleaner, and more elemental than the busier lakes.
- **Practical / Supra notes:**  
  Stay on sensible paved parking / pull-off choices. Don’t get tempted into rougher lakeside edges.

## Stop 6 — Lake Shoji

![Lake Shoji](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/uncover/images/lake-shoji.jpg)

_Image source: [Lake Shojiko official guide](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/uncover/lake-shojiko.html)_

- **What it offers:** A final quiet lake stop with much less crowd energy than the more famous Fuji viewpoints.
- **Time at stop:** about **10-15 minutes**
- **Cost:** Free.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Stop briefly, take in the calmer mood, and let the route end on something quieter before the return leg.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  It gives the route a proper last note instead of ending abruptly after Lake Motosu.
- **What makes it unique:**  
  It feels understated and much more intimate than the bigger-name Fuji stops.
- **Practical / Supra notes:**  
  Keep this one disciplined. It is meant to be a final punctuation mark, not another major linger.

## Late-Day Strategy

This is the most important behavioral rule for this route:

- **Do not add random extra stops after Lake Shoji.**
- Once you are done there, the route has given you everything it needs to give you.
- At that point, your job is to manage the return, not chase one more Fuji angle.

If traffic is building faster than expected:

- keep `Oishi Park` short
- make one clear lunch decision and stick to it
- do not overstay at `Nenba`
- keep `Lake Shoji` brief

## Why This Route Works Better Than A Basic Fuji Day

On paper, this can look like “just another Fuji Five Lakes route.”

In practice, it works because:

- the route starts with an elevated viewpoint instead of a flat tourist stop
- it includes both **iconic** and **quiet** Fuji moments
- it has one real cultural / village stop
- it has one genuinely weird geological stop
- and it finishes with the west-side lakes that feel calmer and more atmospheric than the busiest part of Kawaguchiko

It is one of the best routes in the guide if your priority is:

- **strong sightseeing**
- **high-quality stops**
- **beautiful Fuji views**
- and **a day that feels more layered than repetitive**

# Hakone Ideas

Initial pass for a roughly 24-hour Hakone stay. The ranking is based on "must do" value for a first-time Hakone visit: iconic scenery, unique Hakone-only experiences, easy fit in a short stay, and how much the stop adds beyond Tokyo/Kyoto/Hakone lodging alone.

Planning note: prices and hours below are planning ranges from current research and should be checked before the day, especially for ropeway/cruise status, Owakudani gas closures, museum closed days, and private onsen booking rules. Images are intentionally small. Most are from official attraction pages or Wikimedia Commons; food items sometimes use a location-context image when a reliable dish-specific image was not available.

## Quick 24-Hour Priority

- **Best default day:** Hakone Tozan Railway / cable car -> Hakone Ropeway -> Owakudani -> Lake Ashi cruise -> Hakone Shrine / Moto-Hakone -> onsen or ryokan dinner.
- **Best museum add:** Hakone Open-Air Museum if you only pick one.
- **Best relaxing add:** Hakone Yuryo, Tenzan, or your ryokan onsen.
- **Best historic add:** Old Tokaido Road + Amazake Chaya.
- **Best weather backup:** POLA Museum, Hakone Museum of Art, Venetian Glass Museum, Hakone Yuryo, or Yunessun.

## Must Do

- **1. Hakone Loop: Tozan Railway, cable car, ropeway, Owakudani, Lake Ashi cruise**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Hakone-ropeway_en.JPG/250px-Hakone-ropeway_en.JPG" width="220" alt="Hakone Ropeway">

  - **What we do:** Use Hakone's classic transit circuit to connect Gora, Sounzan, Owakudani, Togendai, Lake Ashi, and Moto-Hakone.
  - **Why included:** This is the defining first-time Hakone experience because the transportation is part of the attraction.
  - **Price:** Hakone Freepass is usually the best value, approx. JPY 6,100 for 2 days from Shinjuku; individual tickets add up quickly.
  - **Time needed:** 5-7 hours if doing the major stops without rushing.
  - **Practical notes:** Build the day around current ropeway/cruise status. If wind, fog, or volcanic gas disrupts the loop, pivot to museums and onsen.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Navi](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/), [Hakone Navi status](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/status/)

- **2. Owakudani volcanic valley**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Owakudani_Valley_near_Owakudani_Station.JPG/250px-Owakudani_Valley_near_Owakudani_Station.JPG" width="220" alt="Owakudani volcanic valley">

  - **What we do:** Stop at the volcanic valley, see the steam vents, look for Fuji views, and try the black eggs.
  - **Why included:** It is one of the most Hakone-specific landscapes and feels different from the rest of the Japan trip.
  - **Price:** Entry is free; black eggs are usually around JPY 500-600 per bag.
  - **Time needed:** 45-90 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Owakudani can close with little notice due to volcanic gas, wind, or ropeway suspension.
  - **Sources:** [Owakudani Kurotamagokan](https://www.owakudani.com/en/), [Hakone Navi status](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/status/)

- **3. Hakone Ropeway**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Owakudani%27s_cable_car%2C_Japan%3B_October_2017.jpg/250px-Owakudani%27s_cable_car%2C_Japan%3B_October_2017.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone Ropeway gondola">

  - **What we do:** Ride the gondola over the volcanic valley between Sounzan, Owakudani, and Togendai.
  - **Why included:** This is the dramatic scenic ride in Hakone and the easiest way to see the volcanic terrain from above.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 1,500 one-way without a pass; covered by the Hakone Freepass.
  - **Time needed:** 30-60 minutes, plus time at Owakudani.
  - **Practical notes:** Early day is better for visibility. Check same-day status before committing.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Ropeway](https://www.hakoneropeway.co.jp/foreign/en/), [Hakone Navi status](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/status/)

- **4. Lake Ashi sightseeing cruise / pirate ship**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Lake_Ashi-pirate_ship.jpg/250px-Lake_Ashi-pirate_ship.jpg" width="220" alt="Lake Ashi pirate ship">

  - **What we do:** Cross Lake Ashi by the sightseeing cruise between Togendai, Hakone-machi, and Moto-Hakone.
  - **Why included:** It gives the classic lake view and connects the ropeway side of Hakone to the shrine and old-road side.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 1,200 one-way without a pass; covered by the Hakone Freepass.
  - **Time needed:** 30-60 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Usually daytime service only. Do not leave this until late afternoon unless you have checked the last sailing.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Sightseeing Cruise](https://www.hakone-kankosen.co.jp/foreign/en/), [Hakone Navi](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/)

- **5. Hakone Shrine and the Peace Torii**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Torii_on_Lake_Ashi_%40_Hakone_Shrine_%2813776878594%29.jpg/250px-Torii_on_Lake_Ashi_%40_Hakone_Shrine_%2813776878594%29.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone Shrine torii on Lake Ashi">

  - **What we do:** Walk the lakeside shrine grounds, visit the cedar-lined shrine, and see the torii gate in Lake Ashi.
  - **Why included:** It is one of Hakone's most iconic places and pairs perfectly with the cruise.
  - **Price:** Shrine grounds are free.
  - **Time needed:** 45-90 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** The water torii photo line can take 30-60 minutes during crowded periods. The shrine itself is still worth visiting even if skipping the photo queue.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Navi](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/)

- **6. Hakone Open-Air Museum**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/Hakone_Open-air_Museum_20211202-10.jpg/250px-Hakone_Open-air_Museum_20211202-10.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone Open-Air Museum">

  - **What we do:** Walk the outdoor sculpture gardens, visit the indoor galleries, and see the stained-glass Symphonic Sculpture tower.
  - **Why included:** Best single museum choice for most first-time visitors because it feels specific to Hakone's mountain setting.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 2,000 adult.
  - **Time needed:** 2-3 hours.
  - **Practical notes:** Strong choice if the weather is decent. If the Hakone Loop gets compressed, this is the museum most worth preserving.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Open-Air Museum](https://www.hakone-oam.or.jp/en/)

- **7. Ryokan onsen and kaiseki dinner**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Lake_Ashi_%26_Mt_Fuji_%26_Hakone_Shrine.jpg/250px-Lake_Ashi_%26_Mt_Fuji_%26_Hakone_Shrine.jpg" width="220" alt="Lake Ashi and Mount Fuji in Hakone">

  - **What we do:** Use the ryokan baths and, if booked, eat a seasonal multi-course kaiseki dinner.
  - **Why included:** If staying overnight, this may be the most "we are in Hakone" experience of the whole 24 hours.
  - **Price:** Usually bundled into lodging if booked with dinner; varies widely.
  - **Time needed:** 2-3 hours total for bath and dinner, not counting overnight stay.
  - **Practical notes:** Kaiseki dinner times are usually fixed. Do not snack heavily late afternoon if dinner is included.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Navi](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/)

## Strong Contenders

- **8. Hakone Yuryo day-use onsen**

  <img src="https://www.hakoneyuryo.jp/assets/img/common/pt-nav-spa01_en@2x.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone Yuryo onsen">

  - **What we do:** Use the public bath or reserve a private open-air bath near Hakone-Yumoto.
  - **Why included:** Best practical onsen add if we do not have enough ryokan bath time or want a private bath option.
  - **Price:** Public bath approx. JPY 2,000+; private baths cost materially more and vary by room.
  - **Time needed:** 1.5-3 hours.
  - **Practical notes:** Private rooms should be reserved ahead. Good final stop before leaving Hakone-Yumoto.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Yuryo](https://www.hakoneyuryo.jp/english/)

- **9. Tenzan Tohji-kyo**

  <img src="https://tenzan.jp/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/menu_tenzan.png" width="220" alt="Tenzan Tohji-kyo official image">

  - **What we do:** Visit a traditional day-use onsen complex with outdoor baths.
  - **Why included:** More atmospheric and old-school than many convenient station-area options.
  - **Price:** JPY 1,450 adult and JPY 700 child per Tenzan official listing; cash only per the same listing.
  - **Time needed:** 2 hours.
  - **Practical notes:** Official listing shows 9:00-23:00 with final reception at 22:00, no regular closing day except a short mid-December closure. Verify rules before going.
  - **Sources:** [Tenzan](https://tenzan.jp/tenzan/)

- **10. POLA Museum of Art**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/161222_Pola_Museum_of_Art_Hakone_Japan15s3.jpg/250px-161222_Pola_Museum_of_Art_Hakone_Japan15s3.jpg" width="220" alt="POLA Museum of Art">

  - **What we do:** Visit the art collection and forest-integrated museum building.
  - **Why included:** Best pick if we want a calmer, design-forward museum or need an indoor weather backup.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 1,800-2,200 adult depending on exhibition.
  - **Time needed:** 1.5-2.5 hours.
  - **Practical notes:** Better as a deliberate stop than a quick 20-minute add-on.
  - **Sources:** [POLA Museum of Art](https://www.polamuseum.or.jp/en/)

- **11. Old Tokaido Road and cedar avenue**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Old_Tokaido_-01.jpg/250px-Old_Tokaido_-01.jpg" width="220" alt="Old Tokaido Road in Hakone">

  - **What we do:** Walk a short preserved section of the old road between Hakone sights.
  - **Why included:** Gives Hakone historical texture beyond cable cars, boats, and museums.
  - **Price:** Free.
  - **Time needed:** 30-75 minutes depending on segment.
  - **Practical notes:** Pick a short section rather than trying to hike a long route during a 24-hour stay.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Navi](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/)

- **12. Amazake Chaya**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Amasake-chaya_%289412075720%29.jpg/250px-Amasake-chaya_%289412075720%29.jpg" width="220" alt="Amazake Chaya in Hakone">

  - **What we do:** Stop at the historic teahouse for amazake and mochi.
  - **Why included:** Compact, atmospheric, and one of the easiest ways to make the Old Tokaido feel real.
  - **Price:** Snacks and drinks approx. JPY 500-1,000.
  - **Time needed:** 20-45 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Works best as a break during an Old Tokaido or Moto-Hakone section.
  - **Sources:** [Amazake Chaya](https://www.amasake-chaya.jp/)

- **13. Hakone Gora Park**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Gora_Park_-_Hakone%2C_Kanagawa%2C_Japan_-_DSC08378.jpg/250px-Gora_Park_-_Hakone%2C_Kanagawa%2C_Japan_-_DSC08378.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone Gora Park">

  - **What we do:** Walk the landscaped garden, greenhouse, and craft areas.
  - **Why included:** Easy filler if we are already in Gora and want a calm stop without much transit effort.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 650, often free with pass benefits.
  - **Time needed:** 45-90 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** More of a pleasant add-on than a destination anchor.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Gora Park](https://www.hakone-tozan.co.jp/gorapark/en/)

- **14. Hakone Checkpoint**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Hakone_Checkpoint_and_Lake_Ashi.jpg/250px-Hakone_Checkpoint_and_Lake_Ashi.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone Checkpoint and Lake Ashi">

  - **What we do:** Visit the reconstructed Edo-period checkpoint and small museum.
  - **Why included:** Best quick history stop around Lake Ashi.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 500 adult.
  - **Time needed:** 30-60 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Pairs well with Hakone-machi, Moto-Hakone, the shrine, and Old Tokaido.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Checkpoint](https://www.hakonesekisyo.jp/english/)

- **15. Onshi Hakone Park**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/230909_Onshi_Hakone_Park_Hakone_Japan01s3.jpg/250px-230909_Onshi_Hakone_Park_Hakone_Japan01s3.jpg" width="220" alt="Onshi Hakone Park">

  - **What we do:** Walk the former imperial villa grounds and look out over Lake Ashi.
  - **Why included:** Good viewpoint stop if the sky is clear and we want a calmer alternative to shrine crowds.
  - **Price:** Free.
  - **Time needed:** 30-75 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Best when visibility is good. Less essential in fog or rain.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Navi](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/)

- **16. Narukawa Art Museum**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/230909_Narukawa_Art_Museum_Hakone_Japan05s3.jpg/250px-230909_Narukawa_Art_Museum_Hakone_Japan05s3.jpg" width="220" alt="Narukawa Art Museum">

  - **What we do:** See Japanese-style paintings and the famous Lake Ashi / Fuji view from the museum.
  - **Why included:** Strong small museum choice if we are already in Moto-Hakone.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 1,500 adult.
  - **Time needed:** 60-90 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Best value when the view is clear.
  - **Sources:** [Narukawa Art Museum](https://www.narukawamuseum.co.jp/)

- **17. Sengokuhara pampas grass fields**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Hakone_sengokuhara_susuki1.jpg/250px-Hakone_sengokuhara_susuki1.jpg" width="220" alt="Sengokuhara pampas grass fields">

  - **What we do:** Walk the open grass-field path and take in the wide mountain scenery.
  - **Why included:** Good outdoor contrast to the denser Gora and Lake Ashi areas.
  - **Price:** Free.
  - **Time needed:** 30-60 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Peak look is golden autumn. In spring/early summer it is greener and less dramatic, but still pleasant.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Navi](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/)

- **18. Hakone Museum of Art**

  <img src="https://www.moaart.or.jp/wp-content/themes/hkn/assets/images/common/top-shinsenkyo_garden.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone Museum of Art garden">

  - **What we do:** Visit the ceramics museum and moss garden.
  - **Why included:** A quiet, garden-forward museum that works well if we want a slower Hakone day.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 1,430 adult.
  - **Time needed:** 60-90 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Research flagged Thursday closures. Verify current schedule before planning around it.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Museum of Art](https://www.moaart.or.jp/hakone/en/)

- **19. Hakone Venetian Glass Museum**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Hakone_Venetian_Glass_Museum.jpg/250px-Hakone_Venetian_Glass_Museum.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone Venetian Glass Museum">

  - **What we do:** Visit the glass art museum and garden.
  - **Why included:** Visually distinctive and useful as a weather backup or specialty museum pick.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 1,800 adult.
  - **Time needed:** 60-90 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** More niche than Open-Air or POLA, but very photogenic.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Venetian Glass Museum](https://www.hakone-garasunomori.jp/en/)

## Food And Local Experiences

- **20. Owakudani black eggs**

  <img src="https://owakudani.com/wp/wp-content/themes/owakudani/images/tanoshimu/black_deco.jpg" width="220" alt="Owakudani black eggs">

  - **What we do:** Buy the famous black eggs at Owakudani.
  - **Why included:** Signature Hakone snack, easy to do while already at the volcanic valley.
  - **Price:** Usually around JPY 500-600 per bag.
  - **Time needed:** 10-20 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Treat this as a snack, not a meal. Availability depends on Owakudani access.
  - **Sources:** [Owakudani Kurotamagokan](https://www.owakudani.com/en/)

- **21. Yuba-don in Hakone-Yumoto**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/161222_Hakone-Yumoto_Station_Hakone_Japan02s3.jpg/250px-161222_Hakone-Yumoto_Station_Hakone_Japan02s3.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone-Yumoto station area">

  - **What we do:** Eat a tofu-skin rice bowl, with Naokichi commonly recommended as the classic option.
  - **Why included:** Local-feeling meal tied to Hakone's spring-water tofu culture.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 1,200-2,000.
  - **Time needed:** 45-90 minutes, longer if waiting.
  - **Practical notes:** Famous shops can have long waits. Best early lunch or off-peak.
  - **Sources:** [Odakyu Hakone-Yumoto food guide](https://hakonetrip.odakyu-global.com/feature/hakone-yumoto-restaurants-and-itineraries/), [ByFood Hakone guide](https://www.byfood.com/blog/travel-tips/what-to-eat-in-hakone)

- **22. Tofu katsuni at Tamura Ginkatsutei**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Gora_Park_-_Hakone%2C_Kanagawa%2C_Japan_-_DSC08378.jpg/250px-Gora_Park_-_Hakone%2C_Kanagawa%2C_Japan_-_DSC08378.jpg" width="220" alt="Gora area context">

  - **What we do:** Eat the famous tofu cutlet stew in Gora.
  - **Why included:** One of Hakone's best-known destination meals and a good match if we are already near Gora.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 1,500-2,500.
  - **Time needed:** 60-120 minutes with wait.
  - **Practical notes:** Known for queues and ticketing. Not ideal if we are trying to keep the day moving fast.
  - **Sources:** [ByFood Hakone guide](https://www.byfood.com/blog/travel-tips/what-to-eat-in-hakone)

- **23. Hatsuhana soba**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/161222_Hakone-Yumoto_Station_Hakone_Japan02s3.jpg/250px-161222_Hakone-Yumoto_Station_Hakone_Japan02s3.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone-Yumoto station area">

  - **What we do:** Eat soba in Hakone-Yumoto before or after the main sightseeing loop.
  - **Why included:** Practical local meal option near the main gateway station.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 1,200-1,800.
  - **Time needed:** 45-75 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Better as arrival/departure meal than a mid-loop detour.
  - **Sources:** [Odakyu Hakone-Yumoto food guide](https://hakonetrip.odakyu-global.com/feature/hakone-yumoto-restaurants-and-itineraries/)

- **24. Bakery and Table Hakone / footbath cafe**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Lake_Ashi_%26_Mt_Fuji_%26_Hakone_Shrine.jpg/250px-Lake_Ashi_%26_Mt_Fuji_%26_Hakone_Shrine.jpg" width="220" alt="Lake Ashi and Hakone Shrine view">

  - **What we do:** Stop by the Moto-Hakone bakery/cafe for bread, snacks, and the lakeside footbath setting if available.
  - **Why included:** Easy, scenic food break near Hakone Shrine and Lake Ashi.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 500-1,500 depending on snack or meal.
  - **Time needed:** 20-60 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Works best as a light break, not the main meal.
  - **Sources:** [ByFood Hakone guide](https://www.byfood.com/blog/travel-tips/what-to-eat-in-hakone)

  - **Why included:** Lake-specific food idea that fits naturally with Moto-Hakone or Hakone-machi.
  - **Price:** Approx. JPY 1,500-2,200.
  - **Time needed:** 45-75 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Best if we are already eating near Lake Ashi.
  - **Sources:** [ByFood Hakone guide](https://www.byfood.com/blog/travel-tips/what-to-eat-in-hakone)

  - **What we do:** Stop at the Sounzan transfer point for the terrace, footbath, and cloud-themed snacks/drinks if open.

## Nice If Nearby Or Specific Interest

- **28. Hakone Tozan Railway as its own activity**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Hakone_Tozan_Railway_at_Hakone%2C_Japan.jpg/250px-Hakone_Tozan_Railway_at_Hakone%2C_Japan.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone Tozan Railway">

  - **What we do:** Treat the mountain train between Hakone-Yumoto and Gora as a scenic ride rather than just transit.
  - **Why included:** Switchbacks and steep mountain rail make it more memorable than a normal train leg.
  - **Price:** Covered by Hakone Freepass; individual fare varies by segment.
  - **Time needed:** 40-60 minutes.
  - **Practical notes:** Can be crowded during peak periods.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Navi](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/)

- **29. Hakone Komagatake Ropeway**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Hakone_komagatake_ropeway01.JPG/250px-Hakone_komagatake_ropeway01.JPG" width="220" alt="Hakone Komagatake Ropeway">

  - **What we do:** Ride up Mount Komagatake for high views over Lake Ashi and surrounding mountains.
  - **Why included:** Potentially excellent viewpoint if skies are clear.
  - **Price:** Planning range approx. JPY 1,800+ round trip; verify current fare.
  - **Time needed:** 1.5-2.5 hours including access.
  - **Practical notes:** Only worth prioritizing with clear weather. It is not the same as the main Hakone Ropeway.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Navi](https://www.hakonenavi.jp/international/en/)

- **30. Yunessun**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/251122_Hakone_Kowakien_Yunessun_Hakone_Japan01s3.jpg/250px-251122_Hakone_Kowakien_Yunessun_Hakone_Japan01s3.jpg" width="220" alt="Hakone Kowakien Yunessun">

  - **What we do:** Visit the themed hot spring water park with novelty baths.
  - **Why included:** Fun if the group wants something playful instead of a traditional onsen.
  - **Price:** Planning range approx. JPY 2,500-3,500 depending on package; verify current price.
  - **Time needed:** 2-3 hours.
  - **Practical notes:** Lower priority for a classic first-time Hakone visit unless the group specifically wants this vibe.
  - **Sources:** [Hakone Kowakien Yunessun](https://www.yunessun.com/global/en/)

- **31. Mishima Skywalk**

  <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Mishima_Skywalk_North_Facade2023.jpg/250px-Mishima_Skywalk_North_Facade2023.jpg" width="220" alt="Mishima Skywalk">

  - **What we do:** Visit the large suspension bridge with Mt. Fuji views.
  - **Why included:** Big visual payoff on a clear day if our route already heads toward Mishima.
  - **Price:** Planning range approx. JPY 1,100-1,300 adult; verify current price.
  - **Time needed:** 1.5-2.5 hours including detour.

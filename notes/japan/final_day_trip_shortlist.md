- Best iconic Fuji payoff without turning the day into only crowded postcard stops.
- Layers together views, village atmosphere, a lava-tube cave stop, and the quieter west-side lakes.
- Strongest choice if you want the day to feel unmistakably Japanese and photograph beautifully.

## Chichibu / Nagatoro Mountain Escape

## Best Choice By Priority

- **Choose `North Fuji / Deep Lakes` if:** you want the best first-time-Japan scenery and the most iconic sightseeing payoff.
- **Choose `Tokyo Bay Loop` if:** you want the most activities and the route that feels the most packed with "cool Japan trip stuff."
- **Choose `Boso Hidden Loop` if:** you want the best balance of many stops, varied scenery, and a day that does not feel dominated by one huge drive.
- **Choose `Chichibu / Nagatoro Mountain Escape` if:** you want a more local-feeling mountain day with good variety and less driving burden than `Gunma`.
- **Choose `Gunma / Usui / Karuizawa / Haruna` if:** the car itself is a big part of the dream, and you are willing to spend most of the day driving for the most ambitious road-trip version.

## Overnight Parking In Shinjuku

If you decide to park overnight in `Shinjuku`, do **not** assume the rental company is automatically fine with a next-morning return.

- `OnlyJDM`'s rules still expect return during business hours at the assigned store.
- Their late-return fee has previously been listed at about **JPY 3,300 per 30 minutes**, so if you are intentionally returning the next morning, it is worth confirming this plan with them in advance.

For the current car, the published size limits below are all workable for a `GR Supra`-sized car. The biggest practical differences are:

- whether the lot is truly **24-hour in/out**
- whether it is on the **east / Kabukicho side** or the **west / Nishi-Shinjuku side**
- and whether it is a **simple self-park** or an **attended structure**

### Option 1 — Public Shinjuku Parking

- **Address:** `2-10-1 Kabukicho, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo`
- **Best for:** the strongest all-around central `Shinjuku` option, especially if you are ending on the east side
- **Published hours:** open daily, but **closed on Sundays / holidays from 12:00 AM to 8:00 AM**
- **Published pricing:** **JPY 1,500 per 12 hours** for a normal car
- **Hotel-linked pricing:** **JPY 2,500 per 24 hours** if you are staying at one of its partner nearby hotels and can show proof
- **Vehicle limits:** height **2.1 m**, width **2.1 m**, length **5.0 m**, weight **1.9 t**
- **Parking type:** attended multi-level self-drive structure; staff **hold the key**
- **Why it is good:** central, established, and relatively inexpensive for the location
- **Main caveat:** the Sunday / holiday overnight closure matters, and key handover may be less ideal if you strongly prefer keeping the key yourself

### Option 2 — Times Nishi-Shinjuku 4-chome No. 4

- **Address:** `4-21 Nishi-Shinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo`
- **Best for:** the cleanest true **24-hour in/out** fallback on the west side of `Shinjuku`
- **Published hours:** **24 hours**
- **Published pricing:**  
  - **Mon-Sat:** **JPY 2,200 max** for `8:00 AM-6:00 PM` plus **JPY 500 max** for `6:00 PM-8:00 AM`  
  - **Sun / holidays:** **JPY 1,400 max** for `8:00 AM-6:00 PM` plus **JPY 500 max** for `6:00 PM-8:00 AM`
- **Typical overnight read:** if you park at night and leave early the next morning, the night portion is very reasonable; if the car stays into the daytime, expect the daytime cap to apply too
- **Vehicle limits:** height **2.1 m**, width **1.9 m**, length **5.0 m**, weight **2.5 t**
- **Parking type:** outdoor self-park
- **Why it is good:** easy overnight logic, west-side location, no overnight lockout window
- **Main caveat:** only **15 spaces**, and it is **cashless only**

### Option 3 — Times Shinjuku Park Tower

- **Address:** `3-7 Nishi-Shinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo`
- **Best for:** a larger west-side garage if you expect to arrive before late evening
- **Published hours:** garage is listed in `Times` as a parking facility with **24-hour parking**, but **general entry/exit is only from 7:00 AM to 11:30 PM**
- **Published pricing:** **JPY 2,100 max** for `7:00 AM-8:00 PM`
- **Vehicle limits:** height **2.1 m**, width **1.9 m**, length **5.0 m**, weight **2.5 t**
- **Parking type:** large structured garage
- **Why it is good:** large capacity compared with many smaller lots, and a strong option if you are staying on the west side
- **Main caveat:** this is **not** the best choice if you may need to arrive very late or move the car overnight; the overnight lockout matters. `Times` notes that hotel guests have a separate 24-hour entry option from the Central Park side

### Option 4 — NPC24H Higashi-Shinjuku Parking

- **Address:** `7-26 Shinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo`
- **Best for:** true 24-hour east-side backup if the main `Kabukicho` option is full
- **Published hours:** **24 hours**
- **Published pricing:**  
  - **Mon-Sat:** same-day max **JPY 3,500**  
  - **Sun / holidays:** same-day max **JPY 2,100**  
  - **Night max (`8:00 PM-8:00 AM`): JPY 1,100**
- **Vehicle limits:** height **2.3 m**, width **1.9 m**, length **5.0 m**, weight **2.0 t**
- **Parking type:** flat self-park
- **Why it is good:** simple rules, true 24-hour operation, and no structured-garage timing caveat
- **Main caveat:** only **2 spaces**, so this is better treated as a backup than as the default plan

### My Practical Parking Picks

If you want the simplest shortlist of actual overnight choices:

1. **`Public Shinjuku Parking`** if you want the most central and best-established east-side option
2. **`Times Nishi-Shinjuku 4-chome No. 4`** if you want the cleanest west-side true 24-hour fallback
3. **`Times Shinjuku Park Tower`** if you are staying on the west side and know you will arrive before the late-night entry cutoff

If I were trying to avoid parking stress, I would **pick the parking lot based on the hotel side of Shinjuku first**, not based on the absolute cheapest headline price.

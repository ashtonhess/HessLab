# Haneda To Mount Fuji Day Trip

A standalone route guide for a **Tuesday, April 28, 2026** self-drive day from **OnlyJDM Haneda Main Store** to the Mount Fuji area.

This guide is built around your actual constraints:

- **Pickup:** `10:00 AM`
- **Store:** `OnlyJDM Haneda Main Store`, `2 Chome-20-14 Omoriminami, Ota City, Tokyo 143-0013`
- **Business hours:** treat **10:00-18:30** as the safe pickup/return window
- **Goal:** a **Mount Fuji-centered** road trip with good mountain driving, scenic stops, towns, activities, maybe a short hike, and enough structure that you could actually follow it on the day

## Why This Guide Exists

Most Tokyo-to-Fuji day-trip content assumes:

- a much earlier start than `10:00 AM`
- bus or train transport
- or a very rigid checklist of crowded stops

That is **not** your situation.

You are picking up at Haneda at `10:00 AM` on the **day before Showa Day / the stronger Golden Week holiday flow**. That means this guide still has to be realistic about:

- outbound traffic
- when Fuji visibility is best
- what actually fits in an **8-hour** vs **10-hour** day
- and what to do if the **10-hour** plan naturally ends too late for same-day return to the store

## OnlyJDM Haneda Logistics You Need To Know

- **Store location:** `2 Chome-20-14 Omoriminami, Ota City, Tokyo 143-0013`
- **Store hours:** commonly shown as **10:00-18:30**
- **Late return fee:** about **JPY 3,300 / $20.71 per 30 minutes**
- **Official pickup/return rule:** returns are expected during business hours at the assigned location

Practical implication:

- the **8-hour** Fuji plan is designed to get you back to Haneda on time
- the **10-hour** Fuji plan is designed to get you back to **Shinjuku** around dinner time and return the car the next morning, **but only if you coordinate that with OnlyJDM**

If you think you may miss `6:30 PM`, contact them before the trip rather than improvising after the fact.

## What Commercial Mount Fuji Day Trips Usually Do

I benchmarked the common day-trip packages out of Tokyo, especially the Fuji Five Lakes / north Fuji ones.

### The recurring high-value stops

These are the places that show up over and over because they really are good:

- `Arakurayama Sengen Park / Chureito Pagoda`
- `Oishi Park`
- `Oshino Hakkai`
- `Mt. Fuji 5th Station`
- sometimes `Gotemba Outlets`

### The common weak points

Package tours often make the day feel full in the wrong way:

- too much time in buses
- too much time lost to crowd choreography
- lunch at tourist-heavy convenience stops
- shopping time at `Gotemba Outlets`
- too many postcard stops and not enough actual activities

### What I’m borrowing from the tours

- they are right that `Chureito` and `Oishi Park` are high-value
- they are right that north-Fuji stops photograph well and read well as a “Japan trip” day

### What I’m intentionally not copying

- the `Gotemba Outlets` trap
- rigid “5th station or bust” thinking
- overstuffing `Oshino Hakkai` into a holiday route

## The Two Versions

This guide gives you:

1. **8-hour option**  
   The realistic “we can still get the car back to Haneda on time” route.

2. **10-hour option**  
   The better, fuller Fuji day. It is stronger as a trip, but almost certainly means **Shinjuku parking overnight + next-morning return**.

## Tuesday 4/28 Reality Check

This is **not** Showa Day yet, which helps. But it is still the day immediately before the holiday, so you should expect some early outbound leisure movement toward the Fuji area.

That means:

- `Chuo Expressway` is still the main structural risk if you need perfect timing
- `Tomei` can also build up, but the full holiday pattern should be lighter than it will be on 4/29
- a very early Tokyo start would still be cleaner, but you are not trapped in the same holiday timing as the next day
- a `10:00 AM Haneda` start still means you are arriving into the Fuji area later than ideal, so the route needs to stay fairly tight

## Which Option I Actually Like Better

- **If you care about getting the car back cleanly the same day:** choose the **8-hour** option
- **If you care about the better overall Fuji trip:** choose the **10-hour** option

The 10-hour version is much closer to what you probably mean by “a proper Mount Fuji car day.”

## 8-Hour Option

### 8-Hour Option Intent

This is the **best realistic Haneda-start Fuji sampler** that still gives you:

- one short hike
- one town / food stop
- one classic postcard stop
- one proper activity

without pretending you can do the entire Deep Lakes itinerary and still get back to Haneda by `6:30 PM`.

### Quick Stats

- **Target window:** about **10:00 AM to 6:00 PM**
- **Total trip time:** about **8h 00m**
- **Total driving time:** about **4h 30m**
- **Core route:** `Haneda -> Arakurayama / Chureito -> Fujiyoshida lunch -> Mt. Fuji Panoramic Ropeway -> Oishi Park -> Haneda`

### Route Shape

`OnlyJDM Haneda -> Chuo / north-Fuji side -> Chureito Pagoda -> Fujiyoshida lunch -> Mt. Fuji Panoramic Ropeway -> Oishi Park -> return to Haneda`

### Why this version works

- it keeps the number of major stops realistic
- it gives you one **iconic short hike**
- one **proper food stop**
- one **activity stop**
- and one **classic scenic stop**

This is the best balance between:

- getting a real Fuji day
- and not turning the return to Haneda into a panic

### 8-Hour Schedule

- **10:00-12:30 PM**  
  Pick up the car at Haneda and drive toward the Fujiyoshida side.

- **12:30-1:20 PM — Stop 1: Chureito Pagoda / Arakurayama Sengen Park**  
  The short hike and the big iconic Fuji shot.

- **1:20-1:35 PM**  
  Drive toward Fujiyoshida lunch.

- **1:35-2:20 PM — Stop 2: Fujiyoshida lunch**  
  Real local food, not tourist filler.

- **2:20-2:40 PM**  
  Move to the ropeway side.

- **2:40-3:40 PM — Stop 3: Mt. Fuji Panoramic Ropeway**  
  The main active stop of this shorter version.

- **3:40-4:00 PM**  
  Move to `Oishi Park`.

- **4:00-4:25 PM — Stop 4: Oishi Park**  
  Clean Fuji-and-lake payoff before leaving the area.

- **4:25-6:00 PM**  
  Begin return to Haneda.

### Stop 1 — Chureito Pagoda / Arakurayama Sengen Park

![Chureito Pagoda](http://fujiyoshida.net/image/rendering/attraction_image/17/keep/1200?v=2117c7110fbe2f9218fdaa7205f8d756b89a04c0)

_Image source: [Arakurayama Sengen Park official page](https://fujiyoshida.net/en/see-and-do/12)_

- **What it offers:** The most iconic “Fuji + pagoda + city” composition in the region.
- **Time at stop:** about **45-50 minutes**
- **Cost:** Parking can be **JPY 1,000-1,500 (~$6.28-$9.41)** depending on lot / timing; the park itself is free.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Park, climb the stairs, get the famous view, and come back down without trying to make this a half-day event.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  If you are only doing one “famous classic” stop, this is the one.
- **What makes it unique:**  
  This is the most instantly recognizable Mount Fuji tourism image for a reason.
- **Practical note:**  
  Parking and local roads can still be busy on a pre-holiday date, so this stop should stay efficient.

### Stop 2 — Fujiyoshida Lunch

- **What it offers:** The best realistic lunch anchor in the shorter route.
- **Time at stop:** about **40-45 minutes**
- **Cost:** `Yoshida Udon` is usually about **JPY 500-1,000 (~$3.14-$6.28)** for a bowl; fuller lunches can run higher.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Eat something local, filling, and fast enough to keep the route moving.
- **Best lunch move:**  
  `Yoshida Udon`
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  It gives the day a real local-food identity.
- **What makes it unique:**  
  `Yoshida Udon` is one of the region’s clearest local specialties and is especially well-suited to a driving day.

### Stop 3 — Mt. Fuji Panoramic Ropeway

![Mt. Fuji Panoramic Ropeway](https://www.mtfujiropeway.jp/cqotof0000000con-img/main_panel_pc20220330.jpg)

_Image source: [Mt. Fuji Panoramic Ropeway official site](https://www.mtfujiropeway.jp/en/)_

- **What it offers:** A compact, high-payoff activity with excellent lake and Fuji views.
- **Time at stop:** about **50-60 minutes**
- **Cost:** Ropeway round-trip is about **JPY 1,300 / $8.16** for adults.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Ride up, do the main viewpoint, enjoy the summit atmosphere, and come back down without overcomplicating the stop.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  It makes the 8-hour route feel like more than just a Fuji-view sampler.
- **What makes it unique:**  
  It’s one of the easiest ways to add a proper “do something” stop without blowing up the day.

### Stop 4 — Oishi Park

![Oishi Park](https://en.kawaguchiko.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/0410.jpg)

_Image source: [Oishi Park official page](https://en.kawaguchiko.net/park-en/oishi-park/)_

- **What it offers:** The cleanest “Fuji + lake + foreground” composition in this shorter route.
- **Time at stop:** about **20-25 minutes**
- **Cost:** Free.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Park, walk out, take the clean postcard-style Fuji photos, and go.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  It gives the route one polished final scenic payoff.
- **What makes it unique:**  
  It is the most effortless high-value photo stop in the day.

### 8-Hour Swap-Ins

If timing, crowds, or interest push you to vary the shorter plan:

- **Swap in `Oshino Hakkai`** for `Oishi Park` if you want water / village atmosphere and don’t mind crowds.
- **Swap in `Lake Yamanaka / Panorama-dai`** if the east-side Fuji view is looking especially clear.
- **Swap in more time at `Chureito`** if you care more about the hike and view than the ropeway.

## 10-Hour Option

### What this version is trying to do

This is the **better Fuji trip**.

It is the one that feels like:

- a proper mountain-and-lakes day
- a more layered sightseeing day
- and the stronger answer to “why did we rent a car for Fuji?”

It is also the one most likely to put you back in **Shinjuku after the store is closed**, which is why the parking fallback matters.

### 10-Hour Quick Stats

- **Target window:** about **10:00 AM to 8:00 PM**
- **Total trip time:** about **10h 00m**
- **Total driving time:** about **5h 15m to 5h 30m**
- **Core route:** `Haneda -> Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya -> Oishi Park -> Lake Saiko / Nenba -> Narusawa Ice Cave -> Lake Motosu -> Lake Shoji -> Shinjuku overnight parking`

### 10-Hour Route Shape

`OnlyJDM Haneda -> Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya -> Oishi Park -> Lake Saiko / Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba -> Narusawa Ice Cave -> Lake Motosu -> Lake Shoji -> return toward Shinjuku`

### 10-Hour Strengths

- It borrows the strongest pieces from the best north-Fuji / Deep Lakes research.
- It avoids the weaker package-tour habits like wasting time at `Gotemba Outlets`.
- It has:
  - one elevated pass stop
  - one postcard stop
  - one cultural village stop
  - one cave / geology stop
  - and two quiet western lakes

This is the version that feels the most **special**.

### 10-Hour Schedule

- **10:00 AM-1:00 PM**  
  Pick up at Haneda and drive toward `Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya`.

- **1:00-1:35 PM — Stop 1: Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya**  
  Elevated Fuji-and-lake opening stop.

- **1:35-2:00 PM**  
  Move to `Oishi Park`.

- **2:00-2:25 PM — Stop 2: Oishi Park**  
  Quick clean postcard stop.

- **2:25-3:10 PM**  
  Move to `Lake Saiko / Nenba`.

- **3:10-4:00 PM — Stop 3: Lake Saiko / Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba + late lunch / early dinner style meal**  
  Cultural stop plus real meal.

- **4:00-4:25 PM**  
  Move to `Narusawa Ice Cave`.

- **4:25-4:50 PM — Stop 4: Narusawa Ice Cave**  
  Cave / lava-tube reset.

- **4:50-5:20 PM**  
  Move to `Lake Motosu`.

- **5:20-5:40 PM — Stop 5: Lake Motosu**  
  Main west-lake payoff.

- **5:40-5:55 PM**  
  Move to `Lake Shoji`.

- **5:55-6:10 PM — Stop 6: Lake Shoji**  
  Final quiet mood stop.

- **6:10-8:00 PM**  
  Return toward `Shinjuku`.

### Stop 1 — Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya

![Misaka Pass / Tenka-chaya](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/recover/images/the-teahouse-in-the-sky-misaka-pass.jpg)

_Image source: [The Teahouse in the Sky, Misaka Pass](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/recover/teahouse-in-the-sky.html)_

- **What it offers:** One of the best elevated Fuji-and-lake viewpoints on the north side.
- **Time at stop:** about **25-35 minutes**
- **Cost:** Stop itself is effectively free; tea / dango / light food spend is optional.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Pull in, take in the high Fuji view, stretch, and use it as the emotional opening move of the route.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  It starts the day with something scenic, atmospheric, and unmistakably Fuji.

### Stop 2 — Oishi Park

![Oishi Park](https://en.kawaguchiko.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/0410.jpg)

_Image source: [Oishi Park official page](https://en.kawaguchiko.net/park-en/oishi-park/)_

- **What it offers:** Clean, polished Fuji-and-lake framing.
- **Time at stop:** about **20-25 minutes**
- **Cost:** Free.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Use this as a fast scenic punctuation mark, not a lingering stop.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  It gives the route one classic polished Fuji moment before the more atmospheric west-lake section.

### Stop 3 — Lake Saiko / Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba

![Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba](https://saikoiyashinosatonenba.jp/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/DSC3030-1024x512.jpg)

_Image source: [Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba official site](https://saikoiyashinosatonenba.jp/en/aboutus/)_

- **What it offers:** The quieter, more atmospheric and cultural side of the Fuji Five Lakes.
- **Time at stop:** about **45-60 minutes**
- **Cost:** `Nenba` is about **JPY 500 / $3.14** for adults and **JPY 250 / $1.57** for children.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Walk the village, slow down a little, and make this the real meal anchor of the day if timing works.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  This is where the route becomes more than “just Fuji views.”

### Stop 4 — Narusawa Ice Cave

![Narusawa Ice Cave](https://www.mtfuji-cave.com/contents/common/images/ice_cave/title_image_en.jpg)

_Image source: [Narusawa Ice Cave official page](https://www.mtfuji-cave.com/en/ice_cave/)_

- **What it offers:** A lava-tube / ice-cave reset in the middle of a scenery-heavy day.
- **Time at stop:** about **20-25 minutes**
- **Cost:** About **JPY 350 / $2.20** for adults and **JPY 200 / $1.26** for children.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Go in, do the loop, and use it to break the day up with something tactile and different.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  It keeps the route from becoming repetitive.

### Stop 5 — Lake Motosu

![Lake Motosu](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/discover/images/lake-motosu.jpg)

_Image source: [Lake Motosuko official guide](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/discover/lake-motosuko.html)_

- **What it offers:** The deepest and most iconic of the west-side lakes in this route.
- **Time at stop:** about **15-20 minutes**
- **Cost:** Free.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Use this as the big western-lake payoff and the emotional climax of the loop.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  It has the strongest “deep Fuji day” feeling.

### Stop 6 — Lake Shoji

![Lake Shoji](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/uncover/images/lake-shoji.jpg)

_Image source: [Lake Shojiko official guide](https://www.yamanashi-kankou.jp/english/uncover/lake-shojiko.html)_

- **What it offers:** A quiet final lake mood instead of one more crowded attraction.
- **Time at stop:** about **10-15 minutes**
- **Cost:** Free.
- **What we actually do here:**  
  Keep it short and let the route finish with atmosphere rather than one more long stop.
- **Why this stop is worth it:**  
  It gives the route a proper closing note.

### 10-Hour Dinner Strategy

This version is much better if you do **not** force a same-day Haneda store return.

Recommended strategy:

- eat the main “real meal” around `Lake Saiko / Nenba`
- treat the Shinjuku return as a **post-drive park + settle in** move
- if you still want dinner after parking, keep it simple near where you stay

If timing slips badly:

- do not add extra Fuji stops after `Lake Shoji`
- start the return earlier
- accept that the main route already gave you the value

## Overnight Parking Fallback Near Shinjuku

This is specifically for the **10-hour** version if you are returning to `Shinjuku` after the store has already closed.

Important note:

- **OnlyJDM officially expects return during business hours at the assigned store**
- late return is about **JPY 3,300 / $20.71 per 30 minutes**
- if you intend to bring the car back the next morning, contact them first rather than assuming it is automatically fine

### Best cheap-ish practical option

#### Kabukicho Kobanmae Parking

- **Address:** `1-20-1 Kabukicho, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo`
- **Why it is useful:** Open 24 hours, central, and explicitly positioned for longer stays
- **Known pricing:** about **JPY 2,200 / $13.81 for 24 hours** from local operator-style listing
- **Why I like it for this use:** It is one of the clearer “overnight in Shinjuku without spending a fortune” options
- **Caveat:** The price point came from a local operator-style listing / practical guide rather than the cleanest multilingual official parking page, so treat it as a strong working estimate rather than absolute law

### Stronger official-feeling fallback

#### Public Shinjuku Parking

- **Address:** `2-10-1 Kabukicho, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo`
- **Why it is useful:** Staffed, established, central, and easy to cite from `s-park`
- **Known pricing:** about **JPY 1,500 / $9.41 for 12 hours** for a normal car based on `s-park` listing
- **Caveat:** Holiday overnight access windows are more complicated, so this is less clean than the Kabukicho option if you want pure overnight simplicity

## What Package Tours Taught Us

The package-tour benchmarking reinforced a few things:

- `Chureito` and `Oishi Park` are popular for good reason
- `Oshino Hakkai` is strong visually but crowd-fragile
- `Gotemba Outlets` is usually a time sink for this kind of day
- west-side lakes and deeper north-Fuji stops make better use of a self-drive plan than repeating the biggest bus-tour cliches

# AI Photo And Video Tools For Trip Libraries

Current as of May 3, 2026.

## Quick Recommendation

- Best all-around paid photo workflow:
  - Use `Adobe Lightroom + Photoshop` if you want the most complete mainstream workflow for importing, rating, cropping, batch editing, object removal, RAW processing, and final exports.
  - Why: Lightroom is still the strongest end-to-end catalog/edit/export system, and Photoshop is the strongest precision editor.
  - Watch out for: subscription pricing, cloud storage tiers, and Adobe generative-credit limits.
  - Image/video: primarily photo-first; video support exists in the Adobe ecosystem, but serious video work belongs in Premiere Pro or another video editor.

- Best local-first workflow for a MacBook Pro M2 Max:
  - Use `Apple Photos + Excire Foto or Mylio + Pixelmator Pro or Photomator + Upscayl`.
  - Why: this keeps most organization and editing local, gives you AI search/culling, and avoids sending an entire trip library to a cloud service.
  - Image/video: strong for images; Excire, Mylio, Apple Photos, PhotoPrism, and Immich can handle videos for organization. Pixelmator Pro can enhance photos and videos, but it is not a full video editor.

- Best free/local starter kit:
  - `Apple Photos Clean Up` for quick object removal on supported Macs.
  - `Draw Things` for free local AI image and video generation.
  - `DiffusionBee` for simpler local Stable Diffusion image workflows.
  - `Upscayl` for free local image upscaling.
  - `DaVinci Resolve Free` for serious video editing without paying upfront.

- Best video workflow:
  - Use `DaVinci Resolve` as the main editor.
  - Add `Topaz Video` if you need local enhancement, upscaling, stabilization, or restoration.
  - Add `Runway` or `Luma` for generative video and AI video experiments.
  - Add `Descript` if the trip videos include voiceover, talking clips, interviews, or vlog-style narration.
  - Add `CapCut` if the goal is fast Reels/TikTok/social edits.

## Best Workflow Stacks

- Practical "whole trip" photo stack:
  - Ingest and organize with `Mylio`, `Excire Foto`, or `Lightroom`.
  - Cull duplicates, blurry shots, similar bursts, and weak compositions with `Excire Foto`, `Mylio`, or Lightroom ratings.
  - Batch crop/color/straighten in `Lightroom`, `Photomator`, `Pixelmator Pro`, `Luminar Neo`, or `ON1 Photo RAW`.
  - Rescue bad shots with `Topaz Photo`, `DxO PureRAW`, `DxO PhotoLab`, or `ON1 Resize AI`.
  - Do creative/generative edits with `Photoshop`, `Luminar Neo`, `Canva`, `Google Photos`, or local tools like `Draw Things`.

- Best private/local stack:
  - `Excire Foto`: local AI culling, search, similarity, duplicates, faces, aesthetic scoring, and video support.
  - `Pixelmator Pro`: one-time Mac app for ML Enhance, Super Resolution, Denoise, Deband, Crop, Repair, background removal, and photo/video enhancement.
  - `Photomator`: better for batch photo editing, RAW workflows, iCloud/Photos integration, AI selections, repair, denoise, and Super Resolution.
  - `Apple Photos Clean Up`: built-in object removal on supported Apple Intelligence devices.
  - `Upscayl`: free/open-source upscaling for selected images.

- Best pro subscription stack:
  - `Lightroom 1TB` or `Photography 1TB` for organization and editing.
  - `Photoshop` for precision generative/object edits and layered work.
  - `Premiere Pro` for Adobe-based video workflows.
  - Best if you want the broadest pro ecosystem and do not mind subscriptions.

- Best quick/social stack:
  - `Google Photos` or `Apple Photos` for simple cleanup and mobile-first edits.
  - `Canva` for quick collages, social posts, AI photo cleanup, and lightweight video.
  - `CapCut` for fast vertical video, captions, templates, and social publishing.

## Free Options Worth Trying First

- `Apple Photos Clean Up`
  - Free with supported Apple Intelligence devices.
  - Best for: quick object removal in Photos.
  - Limitation: requires supported hardware/software and is not a full batch editor.

- `Google Photos`
  - Free AI editing tools available to Google Photos users, plus storage caveats.
  - Best for: quick mobile cleanup, search, sharing, and Magic Editor/Eraser.
  - Limitation: cloud-first and not ideal for full RAW workflows.

- `DaVinci Resolve Free`
  - Best for: serious video editing without paying upfront.
  - Limitation: Studio-only features include many of the strongest AI tools.

- `Draw Things`
  - Best for: local/offline AI image and video generation on Mac/iOS.
  - Limitation: AI generation/editing is powerful but more experimental than a normal photo editor.

- `DiffusionBee`
  - Best for: simple local Stable Diffusion image generation on Mac.
  - Limitation: less flexible than ComfyUI or Draw Things.

- `ComfyUI`
  - Best for: advanced, repeatable, local AI image/video pipelines.
  - Limitation: technical learning curve.

- `Upscayl`
  - Best for: free local image upscaling and batch upscaling.
  - Limitation: not a full photo editor.

- `Immich` / `PhotoPrism`
  - Best for: private self-hosted photo/video libraries.
  - Limitation: require Docker/server maintenance.

  - `Excire Foto` if local/private AI culling and search are the priority.

## Caveats

- Pricing changes quickly:
  - Adobe, Topaz, Canva, CapCut, Runway, Luma, and Descript all have plan/credit systems that can change by region, billing cycle, and promotions.
  - Verify official pages before subscribing.

- Credits matter for whole-trip processing:
  - Adobe, Runway, Luma, Canva, and Descript use credits, AI allowances, media hours, or generation caps.
  - If you plan to process hundreds or thousands of files, do not assume a free or low-tier plan will be enough.

- Local is better for privacy, but not always easier:
  - `Excire`, `Pixelmator Pro`, `Photomator`, `Topaz`, `DxO`, `Draw Things`, `DiffusionBee`, `ComfyUI`, and `Upscayl` are the strongest local Mac options.
  - `Immich` and `PhotoPrism` are powerful but require a server mindset.

- Photo tools are not always video editors:
  - Many photo apps can organize or lightly handle videos, but only `DaVinci Resolve`, `Premiere Pro`, `Final Cut Pro`, `CapCut`, `Descript`, and similar tools are real video editors.

- MacBook Pro M2 Max is strong enough for local AI:
  - The M2 Max is a good machine for local photo enhancement, local Stable Diffusion-style workflows, local upscaling, and video editing.
  - Heavy video AI enhancement and generative video can still be slow locally; cloud tools may be faster but cost more and reduce privacy.
